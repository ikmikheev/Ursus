For this project, we will study the Ursus americanus (black bear) species and investigate patterns of genetic diversity and population structure. Our main question is whether individuals cluster by geography and population, and whether they are closely related (e.g., siblings or parent-offspring). Our dataset is composed of 150 samples distributed across the state of California and a reference genome downloaded from NCBI. We plan to access the Dartmouth HPC, which will allow us to assess the biodiversity of the sequenced species (average 10x depth). From these results, we aim to gain insight into the population structure of any geographic barriers that could disrupt geneflow in the species and result in subpopulations.
